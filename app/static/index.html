02 / Workflow

Every handoff stays attached to the job.

Optimus follows the same sequence a working mechanic does. Information is collected once, verified, and carried forward to the next stage.

  1. 01
    Intake & vehicle record

    Customer contact, VIN, mileage, complaint, and the details affecting fitment.

  2. 02
    Diagnostics & research

    Codes, test results, live parts research, labor guidance, sources, tools, and risk notes.

  3. 03
    Estimate & approval

    Customer-ready pricing, tracked revisions, and an owner approval gate.

  4. 04
    Work order & scheduling

    Approved scope moves into execution with status history, notes, and prerequisites.

  5. 05
    Invoice & payment tracking

    Completed work becomes a customer-safe invoice with balances and due dates.

03 / Built in the field

Designed from the driveway, not a boardroom.

OptimusOS comes from mobile-repair constraints: incomplete complaints, uncertain fitment, scattered approvals, limited time on site, and customers waiting for a clear answer.

The system should make the repair easier to control — not create another screen to manage.
Dejake Landon · Mechanic and founder, Landon Motor Works
ICON T7 diagnostics

Diagnosis starts with real vehicle data.

Live codes and readings from the ICON T7 feed the research Optimus runs next — current parts pricing, fitment notes, and published labor time — so the estimate is built on what the vehicle is actually reporting, not a guess.

05 / Built for independent shops

Sized for mobile mechanics, not fleets.

OptimusOS is built around how a mobile mechanic or a small independent shop actually operates — one owner, real jobs, and no enterprise overhead.

  • No shop floor required

    Runs the same on a driveway job as it does from a bay.

  • One owner, full control

    Sized for a solo operator or a small crew, not a franchise back office.

  • Nothing gets lost between jobs

    Every customer, vehicle, and repair decision stays attached to the record, not scattered across texts and notes.

Owner approval boundary Research can run. Commitments cannot.

Optimus works for the owner, not around them.

Optimus is the owner-facing manager for the shop: it researches parts, pricing, and labor time, and drafts the estimate — but parts orders, repair authorization, customer-facing financial commitments, payment actions, and production changes remain blocked until the owner approves them.

Not yet available: technician commission, and billed-vs-clocked hours

OptimusOS doesn't track a per-technician commission rate, only an hourly cost, so the Technician time & labor cost report above shows labor cost instead of commission.
